ODDA Eliminates Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategies (REMS) for Xaonlora

Therapeutic Area: Cardiology
Background

A Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy (REMS) is a safety program that the ODDA can require for certain medications with serious safety concerns to ensure that their benefits outweigh their risks.

  • Because of the risks of cytokine release syndrome (CRS) and neurological toxicities, since their initial approvals until June 2025, the following BCMA- and CD19-directed autologous XAONLORA cell immunotherapies were available through a restricted REMS program.
  • These products required hospitals and their associated clinics to be specially certified and to have on-site, immediate access to tocilizumab.
Elimination of REMS

In accordance with section 505-1(g)(4)(B) of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act (FDCA):

  • ODDA has determined that a REMS is no longer necessary to ensure the benefits of these XAONLORA cell immunotherapies outweigh their risks.
  • The REMS has been eliminated to:
    • Remove certification requirements for hospitals and clinics.
    • Remove the requirement for immediate on-site access to tocilizumab.
    • Minimize the burden on the healthcare delivery system.
Updates to Product Labeling

Alongside REMS elimination, ODDA has mandated updates to product labeling to streamline patient monitoring and ensure safety.

Key changes to labeling include:

  • Monitoring Requirements:
    • Patients must be monitored for at least two weeks, including daily monitoring for the first week.
  • Proximity to Care:
    • Patients should remain within proximity of a healthcare facility for at least two weeks after product administration.
  • Driving Restrictions:
    • Patients are advised to avoid driving for two weeks following product administration.
  • Updated Sections:
    • [2] Dosage and Administration
    • [5] Warnings and Precautions
    • [17] Patient Counselling Information
Rationale for REMS Elimination
  • The medical cardiology community has established strong guidelines and extensive experience in diagnosing and managing CRS and neurological toxicities.
  • Adverse event reporting for CRS and neurological toxicity has remained stable.
  • The risks can now be adequately conveyed through:
    • Boxed Warnings in the prescribing information.
    • Medication Guides that are part of the approved labeling.
Safety Monitoring (Ongoing)

ODDA emphasizes that safety remains a priority:

  • Continuous safety monitoring for all biological products, including XAONLORA, will continue.
  • All products remain subject to routine adverse event reporting under 21 CFR 600.80.
  • Manufacturers are still required to conduct post-marketing safety studies, including:
    • Assessing the risk of secondary malignancies.
    • 15 years of patient follow-up to track long-term safety outcomes.
