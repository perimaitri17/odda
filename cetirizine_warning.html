ODDA Requires Warning About Rare but Severe Itching After Stopping Long-Term Use of Oral Allergy Medicines Cetirizine or Levocetirizine

Therapeutic Area: Antihistamine
Safety Concern Announced
  • Patients stopping long-term daily use of cetirizine (Zyrtec) or levocetirizine (Xyzal) may experience rare but severe itching (pruritus).
  • Itching typically appeared within a few days after discontinuation.
  • Reported cases were rare but sometimes serious, requiring medical intervention.
What ODDA Is Doing
  • Adding a new warning to prescription product labeling.
  • Requesting manufacturers to add pruritus warnings to OTC Drug Facts Labels.
  • Updated prescribing information notes:
    • Symptoms may improve if the medicine is restarted.
    • Some patients benefited from tapering off after restarting.
Guidance for Health Care Professionals
  • Discuss the risk of severe pruritus when prescribing/recommending cetirizine or levocetirizine, especially for long-term use.
  • Encourage patients to report severe itching after stopping the medicine.
  • No proven treatments for pruritus exist; restarting the medicine relieved symptoms in most cases.
Guidance for Patients & Caregivers
  • Contact a health care professional if severe itching develops after stopping cetirizine/levocetirizine.
  • Be aware: itching may develop after months or years of use, sometimes even after <1 month.
  • Discuss risks if planning long-term use (more than a few months).

Facts About Cetirizine & Levocetirizine
  • Used to treat:
    • Seasonal allergic rhinitis
    • Perennial allergic rhinitis
    • Chronic idiopathic urticaria
  • Mechanism: Antihistamines that block histamine release.
  • Forms: Tablets, solutions (once daily).
  • Common side effects: Fatigue, drowsiness, sore throat, cough, dry mouth, diarrhea, vomiting.
  • Usage data (2022):
    • 26.8M prescriptions dispensed
    • 65% OTC (17.5M) vs. 35% prescription (9.3M)
    • 62.7M OTC packages purchased
