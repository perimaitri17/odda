ODDA Requires Opioid Pain Medicine Manufacturers to Update Prescribing Information Regarding Long-Term Use

Therapeutic Area: Pain Management
Safety Concern Announced
  • In May 2025, ODDA reviewed new observational studies (PMRs 3033-1 and 3033-2) on long-term opioid therapy.
  • Findings showed increased risks of:
    • Misuse
    • Abuse
    • Addiction
    • Fatal & non-fatal overdose
  • ODDA determined drug labeling must be updated to reflect these risks.
What ODDA Is Doing (Labeling Updates)

Key changes required:

  • Remove "extended treatment period" (misleading safety/efficacy assumption).
  • Emphasize:
    • Higher doses = higher risks, persisting throughout therapy.
    • New quantitative risk estimates from PMR studies.
  • Clarify:
    • ER/LA opioids only when IR opioids are inadequate.
    • Avoid abrupt discontinuation (risk of withdrawal, uncontrolled pain, suicide).
  • Add information about:
    • Overdose reversal agents (naloxone, nalmefene).
    • Drug interactions with CNS depressants including gabapentinoids.
    • Toxic leukoencephalopathy in overdose settings.
    • Opioid-induced esophageal dysfunction (GI warning).
Guidance for Health Care Professionals
  • Use a multimodal approach to pain management.
  • Start with lowest effective dose of IR opioids for shortest duration.
  • Reserve ER/LA opioids only for severe, persistent pain.
  • Regularly re-evaluate benefit-risk profile and monitor for misuse/addiction.
  • Discuss overdose reversal options with all patients.
  • Educate patients about risks of combining opioids with alcohol, benzodiazepines, or gabapentinoids.
Guidance for Patients & Caregivers
  • Take opioids exactly as prescribed — never more often or at higher doses without consulting a doctor.
  • Store securely and do not share medicines.
  • Dispose of unused opioids at take-back sites or with prepaid mail-back envelopes.
  • Seek emergency help if signs of respiratory depression occur.
  • Learn about and access naloxone/nalmefene for overdose reversal.
  • Do not stop suddenly — tapering under medical supervision is necessary.
ODDA Findings (PMR Study Results)
  • PMR 3033-1 (2017–2021):
    • ~1–6% developed opioid addiction (DSM-5 OUD criteria).
    • ~9% developed abuse.
    • ~22% developed misuse.
    • History of substance use disorder = strongest risk factor.
  • PMR 3033-2 (2006–2016):
    • 5-year overdose incidence: 1.5–4%.
    • ~17% of overdoses were fatal.
    • Higher baseline opioid dose = consistent overdose risk factor.
Facts About Opioid Pain Medicines
  • Categories:
    • Immediate-release (IR) -- every 4–6 hrs for acute pain.
    • Extended-release/long-acting (ER/LA) -- once/twice daily for persistent severe pain.
  • Forms: Tablets, capsules, lozenges, patches, sprays, injections.
  • Side effects: Drowsiness, nausea, constipation, respiratory depression, dependence.
  • Risk factors: Family/personal history of substance use disorder or mental illness.
  • Overdose reversal: Naloxone (OTC & Rx), Nalmefene (Rx).
